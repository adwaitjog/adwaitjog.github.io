Prerequisites

Students are expected to have a good understanding of the basic computer organization and design. Please talk to the instructor if you do not satisfy this requirement.

Course Description

This is a seminar-type graduate course, where we will discuss research papers on many different topics (e.g., cache or memory systems, scheduling, resource management, micro-architecture, emerging technologies/architectures) in the broad areas of computer architecture and systems. Students are expected to read a variety of papers, critique them, and present them in the front of the class. In addition, students are expected to complete a semester-long research project.

In-Class Presentations

Each student will present maximum three papers throughout the semester. When you present a paper, be prepared to answer a variety of questions asked by the instructor or other fellow students. The goal is to make class lively. If you plan to audit the course, you are required to present at least two papers. A list of papers will be provided to students. They can choose from that list or come up with their own suggestions. Suggestions would need approval from the instructor.

Duration of each class is 80 min. I expect students to first present necessary background (~15 min) and then paper details (~25 min). The remaining (~40 min) will be reserved for discussions driven by the fellow students and the instructor. In short, we brainstorm one paper (including associated issues) per class.

Paper Critiques and Homeworks

All students are required to submit a detailed critique for each paper we discuss in the class. However, the student who presents the paper in-class is not required to submit the critque for that particular paper. Deadline for critique submission is one week from when the paper is discussed in-class.

Each critique should not exceed one-page and must consists of four sections: 1) paper summary, 2) strengths, 3) weaknesses, 4) detailed comments. More details are already discussed in class and associated slides are posted on black-board.

To learn the background material related to each new topic, some homeworks will also be given.

No collaboration is allowed on critiques and homeworks.

Semester-Long Research Project

Students are expected to perform a semester-long research project. All projects need to be approved by the instructor. Students can work in groups if they wish, but not more than 2 students are allowed to be in a single group. Please contact the instructor early to brainstrom potential project topics.
