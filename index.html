About

I am an assistant professor of computer science at The College of William and Mary (W&M) (US News Rankings) in Virginia, USA. At W&M, I am starting a new research group with the goal of designing future computing systems more energy-efficient, reliable, secure, capable, and useful.

I am looking for highly self-motivated, talented, and creative students to work with who have interests in the broad areas of computer architecture, parallel computing, and memory systems. If you have a different background, it is okay, we can still chat and see if there are any mutual research interests.

I am also excited to explore opportunities that can lead to solid cross-disciplinary research work. Therefore, I am also on the lookout for students having interests in other disciplines such as (but not limited to) biology, economics, machine learning, security, and theory.

Feel free to contact me if you are interested to know more or discuss the possibilities of joining my group. Also, feel free to browse through my publication list and let me know if anything interests you.
