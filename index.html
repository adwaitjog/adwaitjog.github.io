About

I am an assistant professor of computer science at The College of William and Mary (W&M) (US News Rankings) in Virginia, USA. At W&M, I have started a new research group with the goal of designing future computing systems more energy-efficient, reliable, secure, capable, and useful.

I am looking for self-motivated, talented, and creative students to work with who have interests in the broad areas of computer architecture. I am also excited to explore opportunities that can lead to solid cross-disciplinary research work. Therefore, I am also looking for students having interests in other disciplines such as (but not limited to) biology, economics, machine learning, security, and quantum physics. Feel free to contact me if you are interested to know more or discuss the possibilities of joining my group. Also, feel free to browse through my publication list to get an idea of the topics I had been working on in the past.
