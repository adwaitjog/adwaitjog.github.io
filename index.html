About

I am a member of High Performance Computing Lab at Penn State, where I work with my advisors Chita Das and Mahmut Kandemir. My research interests lie in the broad areas of computer architecture and systems. Primarily, I am interested in designing performance and energy-efficient GPU-based platforms that are making headway in almost every kind of computing system ranging from warehouse-scale to wearable computers. I am also interested in rethinking the hardware/software interface for GPU-based heterogeneous computing systems and leveraging emerging memory technologies to design memory and storage systems for them. In recognition of my work, I have received the Best Graduate Research Assistant award in 2013 at Penn State (CSE Department News) and was selected as one of 25 finalists for an NVIDIA Ph.D. Fellowship in 2013 (CSE Department News). My research work is also published in the best venues: ASPLOS, ISCA, MICRO, PACT, DAC. In the past, I worked as an intern with NVIDIA Research in the summer of 2013, Intel Labs in the summer of 2012, and Intel Corp. in the summer of 2011. Before joining Penn State in Fall 2009 with College of Engineering Fellowship, I completed my undergraduate studies at NIT Rourkela, India.

Software and Downloads

Mars on GPGPU-Sim
Mars is a MapReduce framework on GPUs. This package contains MapReduce implementations of some applications that can take advantage of CUDA enabled GPU. More details can be viewed here. I decided to run these applications on GPGPU-Sim, a publicly available GPU Simulator. The tarball to help you get started can be found here. Some of these applications are used to evaluate the proposed schemes in our ASPLOS-2013 paper. If you find this tarball useful, please cite our ASPLOS-2013 paper (BibTeX) and Mars.
